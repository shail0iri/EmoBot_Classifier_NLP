## Anxiety Support

Anxiety often shows up as racing thoughts, tight breathing, restlessness, stomach discomfort, or a feeling that something bad may happen. A helpful first step is to slow the body down before trying to solve the problem.

Grounding: Try the 5-4-3-2-1 method. Name 5 things you can see, 4 things you can feel, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste.

Breathing: Try box breathing. Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale for 4 seconds, and hold for 4 seconds. Repeat for 3 to 5 rounds.

Thought check: Ask, "What evidence supports this worry?", "What evidence goes against it?", and "What is one small next step I can take?"

For exams, interviews, presentations, or deadlines, focus on preparation plus recovery. Make a short checklist, practice once, then take a break so the nervous system can settle.

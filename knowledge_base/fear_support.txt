## Fear Support

Fear is the body's alarm system. It can protect you from danger, but it can also become loud when the threat is uncertain or imagined.

Safety check: Ask, "Am I in immediate danger right now?" If yes, move to safety or contact emergency support. If no, focus on calming the body first.

Reality testing: Ask, "What is the most likely outcome?", "What would I do if the feared thing happened?", and "Who could help me?"

Coping statement: "This feels scary, but a feeling is not the same as a fact. I can take one step at a time."

For presentation or performance fear, prepare the first sentence, practice once out loud, and plan a grounding cue such as pressing your feet into the floor.

## Anger Support

Anger can be a signal that a boundary, need, or value feels threatened. The goal is not to pretend anger is wrong, but to respond without causing harm.

Pause: Step away from the situation if possible. Drink water, unclench your jaw and hands, and take slow breaths before replying.

Name the need: Ask, "What do I need right now: respect, space, clarity, fairness, rest, or help?"

Use assertive communication: "I feel upset when this happens because it affects me in this way. I need us to discuss a better next step."

If anger feels physically intense, try progressive muscle relaxation. Tense and release one muscle group at a time, starting from your shoulders or hands.
